JOURNEY THROUGH INDIA

The most well-known traditional Indian dance genres and the states where they originated



Tamil Nadu/South India’s Bharatanatyam


Bharatanatyam, often regarded as the mother of all other classical dance styles, is one of India’s oldest dance genres, having evolved from temple dancers in Tamil Nadu. Dance is a pure mash-up of emotions, music, beat, and rhythm.

Uttar Pradesh/North India’s Kathak


Kathak, a prominent and well-known classical Indian dance form that originated in Uttar Pradesh in northern India, is another well-known and well-known form. This one gets its name from the word katha, which means storey, and the dancers use their eyes and expressions to tell stories throughout the dance.

Kerala/South India’s Kathakali


One of the most gorgeous, dramatic, and elaborate forms of classical Indian dance is this one. Kathakali originated in the 17th century in the south Indian state of Kerala, often known as God’s Own Country. The dancer must wear intricate make-up, hefty clothes, and, most importantly, practice on their gestures.
